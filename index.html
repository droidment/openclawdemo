> what the room sees

A terminal aesthetic with social UX.

The whole point is not just “AI in chat.” It’s giving a group a shared command surface for workflows, summaries, research, and coordination without turning the conversation into mush.

1. Ask in plain language

Kick off a summary, stock workflow, or task from the same thread where the work is happening.

2. Get structured output

Responses come back concise, useful, and shaped for the context — not generic chatbot sludge.

3. Iterate in public

The group sees the prompts, the results, and the improvements in real time, which makes the demo stronger.
